# Japanese Swordsmith

> A realistic Japanese swordsmithing simulation mod for **Minecraft Forge 1.20.1**, designed to integrate seamlessly with **SlashBlade**.

---

## Overview

Japanese Swordsmith is a Minecraft mod that recreates the traditional process of Japanese sword making while maintaining an enjoyable gameplay experience.

Instead of crafting powerful swords instantly, players experience every major step of the swordsmithing process—from gathering raw materials to forging, quenching, polishing, and finally creating a completed blade that can be used within SlashBlade.

The goal of this project is to let players experience the craftsmanship behind Japanese swords rather than simply crafting weapons.

---

## Project Goals

* Recreate traditional Japanese swordsmithing in Minecraft
* Integrate naturally with SlashBlade progression
* Be enjoyable for both beginners and experienced players
* Support both single-player and multiplayer gameplay
* Provide extensive documentation for modders and contributors
* Encourage community-created addons and translations

---

# Features

## Traditional Tatara Steelmaking

* Gather Satetsu (iron sand)
* Produce charcoal
* Build and operate a Tatara furnace
* Smelt Kera (steel bloom)
* Sort Tamahagane from the bloom

---

## Forging

Forge blades through an interactive smithing system.

Features include:

* Heating management
* Hammer positioning
* Steel shaping
* Multiple forging stages
* Blade geometry control

As players gain experience, fewer visual guides are provided, encouraging mastery through observation.

---

## Quenching

Experience differential hardening through a dedicated mini-game.

Players will:

* Apply clay to the blade
* Heat the blade
* Judge temperature by color
* Choose the correct quenching moment
* Temper the blade afterward

The objective is to learn the process—not simply react quickly.

---

## Polishing

Bring the blade to its final form.

Includes:

* Multiple polishing stones
* Edge refinement
* Surface finishing
* Hamon (temper line) reveal

The hamon is generated based on the forging and quenching process rather than selected manually.

---

## Smith Progression

Players improve over time.

### Smith Level

Unlocks:

* New crafting stations
* New techniques
* Advanced recipes
* Additional gameplay mechanics

### Individual Skill Levels

Each process has its own proficiency:

* Tatara
* Forging
* Quenching
* Polishing

A player may excel in one discipline while remaining inexperienced in another.

---

## Multiplayer

Designed for cooperative gameplay.

Players may specialize in different roles:

* Tatara operator
* Blacksmith
* Quenching specialist
* Polisher

Large workshops become efficient through teamwork.

---

## Sword Certificate

Completed swords can be combined with a special certificate paper.

The certificate records:

* Smith name
* Creation date
* Blade information
* Quality data

This allows players to recognize the creator of every blade.

---

## Reforging

Blades are never permanently lost.

Players may:

* Melt unfinished blades
* Melt completed blades
* Recover Tamahagane
* Reforge the blade from scratch

This encourages experimentation rather than punishment.

---

## SlashBlade Integration

Japanese Swordsmith is designed around SlashBlade.

Rather than replacing SlashBlade progression, this mod becomes an alternative production method.

Players must still satisfy SlashBlade progression requirements before crafting advanced blades.

---

## Optional Integrations

Supported (not required):

* SlashBlade (Required)
* JEI
* EMI
* Create
* Jade
* Patchouli

Create integration allows automated bellows and workshop machinery while preserving gameplay balance.

Automation improves efficiency—not blade quality.

---

# Design Philosophy

The project follows four principles.

### Realistic, but Fun

Historical accuracy is respected whenever possible without sacrificing enjoyable gameplay.

### Learn Through Practice

Players gradually rely less on UI assistance and more on observation, just like real swordsmiths.

### Multiplayer First

Working together should feel rewarding, but every system remains fully playable in single-player.

### Open Source

Japanese Swordsmith is designed as a community project.

Contributions, bug reports, translations, documentation improvements, and addons are all welcome.
